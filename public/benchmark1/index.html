Outside-the-Box

Game Design Document
By Sean Moore and Yeongjae (Jake) Jin

Last Updated: March 31, 2026

1. Game Overview

Outside-the-Box is an annoying, brain-teasing puzzle game built around subverting player expectations. Unlike traditional games where mechanics are taught through tutorials, this game challenges the player to think laterally, question assumptions, and interpret problems creatively. Progress is achieved by completing a sequence of exam-like levels using a combination of logic, keyboard precision, and mouse interaction.

2. Technology

Outside-the-Box will be developed for the Windows Platform using Wolfie2D game engine, which is developed by Richard McKenna for 2D games. Web Graphics Libraries (WebGL) will be used as the underlying rendering technology.

3. Story & Setting

In today’s professional world, everyone claims to be an “innovator” and a “problem solver,” but there is no objective way to verify it. The Outside-the-Box (O-t-B) Certification exam was created as the world’s first standardized exam to test true critical thinking ability. Those who pass can officially claim they can “think outside the box” and put any associated skills on their resumes with a certificate to back it up.

4. Objective

To earn the certification, the player must complete every question/level on the exam.
  • Levels must be completed in a pre-set sequence
  • A player is allowed three incorrect answer submissions throughout the exam before failing
  • Failing resets progress to level 1
  • Completing all levels signifies passing the exam and awards the player with the O-t-B certification

5. Gameplay Progression

Outside-the-Box is structured like a certification exam that escalates over time. Early levels introduce the player to the game’s deceptive style, then later levels combine unconventional puzzle solving with mechanical skill challenges. By the end of the exam, the player is expected to think quickly, question assumptions, and adapt to hidden mechanics.

Phase 1: Introduction/Witty Nature

Each level is designed to challenge the player in a different way. The first chunk of questions, roughly five to ten levels, introduces the witty and deceptive nature of the exam.

Phase 2: Mechanical Skill

After the first chunk of brain-teasing questions, the second chunk begins to test the player’s mechanical skill. Players navigate a rectangular arena filled with alphanumeric and symbolic blocks, tasked with arranging them in a designated Answer Zone to solve various equations or word puzzles.

Phase 3: Full Exam Chaos

Once the player is comfortable, the exam becomes much harsher. From that point onward, progress requires a true combination of wit and skill. Every level has its own quirks, but correct answers, wrong answers, and scene transitions will still be reinforced through consistent audio and animation feedback.

Phase 1 Example: Solve the math problem!

You are on question 6. You are asked to solve a complex math problem. After finding the answer to be 6, you will notice none of the answer choices are 6. The player can only progress by clicking the 6 in the part of the HUD that displays the level they are on.

Phase 2 Example: The Maze

You will be put in a maze and chased by an enemy. You must reach the end of the maze without being caught.

Phase 3 Example: Pong!

Around level 8, the player may be asked to play Pong against an AI opponent that is decent but beatable. Later, around level 18, the player faces the AI again. This time the AI has near-perfect ball tracking, making the match feel impossible.

The trick is that the player is no longer limited to the normal controls. To win, they must figure out that the mouse can now be used to grab the AI paddle and hold it in place long enough to score. This level represents the core philosophy of the game: the solution exists, but only for players willing to challenge their own assumptions.

Endgame Page

If the player completes the exam, they will be presented with a summary of their performance and a certificate based on their score.

  • Bronze Certificate: Completed the exam
  • Silver Certificate: Completed the exam within a certain time frame
  • Gold Certificate: Completed the exam within a certain time frame and fulfilled a secret side objective

(The current idea for the secret side objective is to hide a recurring character throughout the exam and require the player to notice and interact with them in every appearance.)

6. Example Levels

Witty / Deceptive Levels

Level 1 – Name Entry

The player is simply asked to enter their name. This seems harmless at first, but the same name must be remembered and entered again in the final level.

Level 2 – Terms of Agreement

The player must agree to a Terms of Service screen filled with meaningless placeholder text. Clicking “Agree” too early costs a life. The only safe way to proceed is to scroll all the way to the bottom, where a message thanks the player for actually reading the terms.

Level 3 – Basic Math

The player is asked a simple question: “What is the square root of 121?” Unlike many other levels, this one is straightforward. The correct answer, 11, is one of the visible choices.

Level 4 – Click the Dot

The prompt reads “Click the dot.” None of the answer choices are actual dots. The real solution is to click the dot above the letter i in the question itself.

Level 5 – Stay Calm

A bomb appears with a countdown timer while a nearby man repeatedly tells the player to stay calm. Wires extend from the bomb, and the cursor becomes a wire cutter. Cutting any wire costs a life. The correct solution is to do nothing and wait for the timer to expire.

Mechanical Skill Levels

Level 8 – Pong

A literal game of Pong. The player must defeat a beatable AI opponent using standard paddle controls.

Level 9 – Typing Test

The player must type a preset paragraph accurately within a time limit. This level tests speed, composure, and keyboard precision under pressure.

Level 10 – The Maze

The player navigates a maze while being chased by an AI demon. Reaching the exit without getting caught clears the level.

Level 11 – Block Combination Tutorial

The player is introduced to the mechanics of combining blocks in the arena. They must arrange blocks to solve a simple equation, such as 2 + 3 = 5, by dragging and dropping the correct blocks into the Answer Zone.

Level 12 – Exploding Blocks

The player must solve a puzzle by combining blocks, but some blocks are unstable and will explode if held for too long. The player must quickly identify and use the stable blocks to complete the equation before time runs out.

Tougher Late-Game Questions

Level 15 – "Number of seconds you have left to solve this"

The player must use countdown block to solve a puzzle. The block gradually counts down from n seconds,and the player must use it at the right moment to complete the equation before it hits zero.

Level 18 – The answer is "Not found".

The player has blocks of alphabets and numbers. It looks like a word puzzle, but the correct answer is actually 404, where all number blocks are hidden as invisible and the player must figure out to use the invisible blocks to solve the question.

Level 19 – Find Gold!

There are multiple answers to this question. The player can submit AU,79,Gold, or a block that is colored gold. Each answer is accepted as correct, but time limit won't allow to think through all the possibilities, so the player must quickly identify one of the correct answers to pass the level.

Level 20 – Empty the box.

The map is filled with heavy blocks that is very difficult to move. The answer zone is also filled with the blocks, so the player must find invisible countdown blocks to blow up everything in the map to clear the level.

Level 21 – Tortoise vs. Hare

The player controls Turbo the Tortoise and must collect letter blocks spelling “TURBO” before the Hare collects blocks spelling “HARE.” The obvious path fails. The winning move is to collect the farthest block, T, first, which grants a speed boost needed to finish in time.

7. Controls

While controls may vary from level to level, the following are the standard controls throughout the exam

  • W – Move up
  • A – Move left
  • S – Move down
  • D – Move right
  • H – Hold a Block
  • Left Click – Interact
  • Esc – Pause

9. Help Screen Content

About the Game

Outside-the-Box is a certification-style puzzle game designed to test true critical thinking. Players must complete a sequence of unconventional levels by questioning assumptions and discovering hidden mechanics.

Developers

Sean Moore and Yeongjae (Jake) Jin

How to Play

  • Read each question carefully — the obvious answer is often wrong
  • Use both mouse and keyboard interactions
  • You are allowed only three mistakes before failing the exam
  • Some solutions may require interacting outside answer choices
